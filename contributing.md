### Best Practices for Effective Diagrams

To ensure your diagrams are clear and effective for everyone, please follow these guidelines:

*   **Add a Legend or Key:** Always include a legend to explain the meaning of specific shapes, colors, line styles, and icons. This prevents misinterpretation.
*   **Maintain Consistency:** Use the same shapes for the same types of components (e.g., rectangles for processes, diamonds for decisions) and the same color scheme across all related diagrams.
*   **Use Clear Labels:** Directly label components on the diagram instead of relying only on a legend. Avoid long paragraphs; use concise text.
*   **Limit Colors & Shapes:** Use a minimal color palette and a standard set of shapes. Too much variety can make the diagram confusing and harder to follow.
*   **Ensure Logical Flow:** Arrange elements to guide the viewer's eye in a logical sequence, typically from top-to-bottom or left-to-right. Use arrows to clearly indicate direction and relationships.
*   **Keep It Simple:** Avoid cluttering the diagram. Focus on conveying the core concept. If a process is complex, consider breaking it down into multiple, simpler diagrams.
